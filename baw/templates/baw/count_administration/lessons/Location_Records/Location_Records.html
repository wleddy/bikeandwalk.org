Location Records

The Location records represent the places where you will be counting traffic.

Location Name

Location Name can be anything you like. It's just a way for you to tell locations apart easily.

Street Names

Here again, you can use any name for the streets that make sense in your neck of the woods.

Notice that the form is requesting the name of the streets running North to South and East to West. This will help counters orient themselves during the count.

Also, form is for an "Intersection" count. See below for details about setting up a location for a Mid-block or "Screen Line" style count.

Screen Line or Intersection?

We support two styles of count, either a "Screen Line" or "Intersection".

A Screen Line count is where the counters will be simply counting the traffic as it passes by. The counters are usually stationed mid block.

In an Intersection count, the counters are stationed at an intersection and they count traffic passing through the intersection from all directions and also recording their turning actions, if any.

Latitude and Longitude

This is where you define the exact geographic location.

You can either enter the latitude and longitude in the fields provided (yeah, I know, not likely) or you can use the handy map we provide. Click on the marker and move it to the correct spot on the map. You can zoom in and out of the map in the usual ways to find the spot.

Note: Don't reuse Location records. It's Ok to move the location marker to correct it, but if you decide to count at another location, even if it's nearby. After a count has actually been done at a location, don't move the marker some place new. Create a new Location record instead.
